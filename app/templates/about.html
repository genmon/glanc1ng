About

What is Glanc1ng?

Glanc1ng helps you sync up with your closest friends, workmates, or significant other. Use Glanc1ng to see when your very closest Twitter friends are active -- if all of them are active, start a conversation. (You'll also get to see when "mutual followers" on Twitter use Glanc1ng - the people you follow who also follow you - and they'll see you.)

When you glance at a friend, you're letting them know you're thinking about them. When your friend glances back, you know they're thinking about you too.

Glanc1ng does not read your tweets, and will not tweet on your behalf. Glanc1ng does use Twitter for sign in, and for your list of friends so that your activity is visible to mutual followers.

Is that it?

Yes. Sorta. Kinda. It's minimal. Glanc1ng is a work in progress, and there's more to come...

Glanc1ng is a teeny weeny experiment in small group dynamics, and working with the smallest - almost ambiguous - possible unit of interaction: Eye contact. Can we use glancing (or Glanc1ng) to strengthen existing social relationships? To choose when to have lunch together, without overt planning? For togetherness? To build intimacy in the crowded social networks, and warmth in the individual isolation of the inbox? Dunno. Let's see what happens, and I'll build more features as they occur to me.

A teeny weeny experiment?

Glanc1ng should be considered "pre-pre-alpha." There's been no effort put into site scaling, and the database isn't being backed up.

What is a glance?

A glance is...

  • Pleasant: It's nice to discover that two of you have glanced at one-another recently.
  • Reciprocal: The only way to see whether someone has glanced at you is to glance at them.
  • Polite by default: You won't notice glances from a person unless you've actively add them to your group. And if you add a person, they won't be prompted to add you back.

How come the Twitter thing?

Twitter provides sign-in and an underlying social network. It's easier than building these things just for this. Glanc1ng doesn't read your tweets, and won't tweet on your behalf. Glanc1ng does look at your friends list, and sends glances to them within the Glanc1ng system -- note that your friends will only notice you glancing if you're mutual followers on Twitter (that is, you follow them and they follow your). If you're not mutual followers, they will only see that they have received an anonymous glance.

What's the history?

I'm Matt Webb. You can find me at @genmon. Way back in 2003/4, I wrote the very first version of this software and gave a talk about it (more notes). This re-imagination - and first public version - is for me to celebrate 10 years since that talk at the O'Reilly Emerging Technology Conference on 11 February 2004. Timequake! Also so I can remember how to code. It's been a while.
